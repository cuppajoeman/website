about me

Currently I am spending much of my time learning and working in a functional programming language called lean, the language allows you to write formal proofs that you find in mathematics and can help you produce the proof as well. Whenever we develop tools to make a domain be machine readable, it opens up many possibilities for advancement in said field. Additionally I am attracted to the idea of building an open source library of mathematical proofs, so that mathematicians can work together and collaborate easier and open up mathematical research and development past the boundaries of universities, and into the hands of anyone who has access to a computer.

I have also developed a new type of notation for music, which is designed to be paired with isomorphic instruments, (think of a guitar) tuned in 4ths. It allows beginners to learn quickly and minimizes the amount of memorization, they need to get started. It also is an analogue of the shift from the just intonation system to the equal temperament system, and allows us to expose more harmonic information as well as understanding dissonance and consonance by using harmonics. This notation is always under development, and is being improved as I figure out what is the best way forward. Here is an example what this notation looks like, I am also working on a paper which describes how to use the system in tandem with an isomorphic instrument and how to convert standard notation into this new notation. Here is a sample of one of the pages where we prove a fundamental property about the equal temperament system before creating some new notation.

I also am working on an open source multiplayer fps game which is modelled around the fps games of the 90's and is mainly inspired by the vision of games like CPMA, DeFRaG and other fast pased skill based fps games. Check out the progress here.

I also enjoy spending time outside and staying active, one of my favorite games to play is a game called chinlone which is an old game which originated from Myanmar which combines the skills of juggling a soccer ball with the mindset of playing hacky sack. You can learn more about it here and learn how to make your own by watching this video. I also enjoy skateboarding.

I also like to have fun and not take things too seriously
